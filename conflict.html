Conflict / Conflict Resolution


Focus Question:
2.3.12.C.3: Predict the societal impact of the intergroup conflict that results when greedy and dishonest politicians get into power and serve themselves rather than the people.

To answer this question I found a song that expresses the feeling of this intergroup conflict very well.

Personal Connection:
I feel a particular connection to this topic because I am now of voting age. I have started paying attention to the presidential political process and it all seems like a circus. I think many other people feel this way too, especially new young voters. Also, since many people my age like rock and roll, this Who song really connects.

Artifact:
The author of the artifact chosen for this topic is the music group, The Who.
The sound track to the song is here YouTube Video
The lyrics to the song is here Song Lyrics

Summary:
I picked this song because it represents my feelings about the conflicts going on in our political system today. The politicians just play us all for fools. The first verse starts out like this:
We'll be fighting in the streets
With our children at our feet
And the morals that they worship will be gone
And the men who spurred us on
Sit in judgement of all wrong
They decide and the shotgun sings the song
It is describing how people are rebelling against those in power. But those in power are the ones who made things bad for the people fighting. They set up the rules, and then punish those who are rebelling.
The song goes on with different descriptions of how things might change with new leaders, like with a new constitution or a new revolution or a new war or new slogans. But behind all that, things are actually just the same.
There's nothing in the streets
Looks any different to me
They are hoping they “don’t get fooled again” with the new boss. The song ends with:
Meet the new boss
Same as the old boss

Analysis:
As long as there are politicians who are looking out for themselves rather than really trying to do good for the people and the country, this conflict will persist. The societal impact on the people in the society is that they feel deceived and distrusting. When people are angry and discouraged by the way their country is run and how they are treated, then it can be very hard for them to think positively, be constructive, and want to do good things themselves. Sometimes it’s just easier to believe what they’re saying and think they’re looking out for everyone’s best interests. But I do believe that some politicians are good people and really are trying. The solution is for people to “not get fooled again” when they are voting. This is easier said than done.

Discussion Questions:
Question 1: Do you think that there is an intergroup conflict between politicians in our country and the people of our country? Why or why not?
Question 2: How can we keep from getting fooled by the people running for office in our country?
