Addiction


Focus Question:
2.2.12.E.1: What options does a family have in seeking treatment for their loved one who is struggling with addiction?

To answer this question I researched different services for helping with addiction.

Personal Connection:
Thankfully, I have not had anyone in my family with an addiction problem. However, there was a family friend that we found out had a bad problem with prescription pain killers. I remember times when I could tell that she was not acting normal or rational.

Artifact:
The author of the artifact chosen for this topic is the "Live and Work Well" web site.
I researched and compared/contrasted health services for addiction, such as health insurance plans, rehab facilities, various types of counseling programs, self-help kits. I did this by going to the web site that I have access to through my parents’ medical insurance plan: Live and Work Well
Even without a login to the site, you can read about interesting topics that are listed on the right.

Summary:
My parents’ health insurance provides a variety of services for addition. I browsed the web site www.liveandworkwell.com and I found an amazing amount of information and help about different types of addictions. There are self-help guides in case you want to figure out if you or someone you know has a serious problem or if you want to try working things out on your own. There are links to several self-help programs including: a tobacco cessation plan, a personal empowerment program called “Recognizing At-Risk Drinking and Drug Use”, and information about eight different smartphone apps for substance use disorder treatment and recovery.

The web site also has sections that provide comprehensive information and further resources for the topics of: alcoholism, children and addiction, drug use, tobacco, and other addictions. There is information on how to contact helpful organizations like Al-Anon, Alateen, AA, Nar-Anon, and Gam-Anon. There is even a phone number to call to get confidential help, 24/7.

When I looked up what kinds of treatments are covered by the insurance, I found both out-patient and in-patient treatments. Out-patient treatments could be at a counsellor’s or doctor’s office or at a hospital. In-patient treatments could be at a hospital or a residential treatment facility.

Analysis:
There are different approaches to helping people with addictions. First, you need to identify if there is an addiction. There are self-help ways of figuring this out, and there are also medical tests that can help determine if you’re addicted. Once you figure out if you or your loved one is addicted to something, then you/they can try self-help ways of quitting, or can reach out to group-help organizations or other therapy. Groups like AA or Nar-Anon can be very effective at helping people. Some people don’t want to deal with their problems in a group, so the self-help tools or individual therapy might be better for them. But in my opinion, having the help and support of other people who are struggling with the same thing can be a very powerful and positive influence. You might discover that other people have similar issues in their lives that led them to want to drink or do drugs, and this could give you new insights on dealing with your own addiction.

Many addictions involve having to get over the body’s physical addition, before the person can have any hope of staying away from the substance they’re addicted to. It is often necessary for the addict to get medical help. A simple example is “the patch” for helping to get off of nicotine. However, for an alcoholic or a narcotics addict to stop taking their drug, the body will go through a painful withdrawal. Since there is a range of choices in medical treatments, from regular office visits, to all-day treatment centers, to residential live-in treatment centers, I think that the necessary level of treatment will depend on how bad the addict’s situation is.
